# Resume writing rules : voice and hard gates

## Hard gates (a violation fails the artifact)
1. No fabrication. Every bullet traces to `config/accomplishment-bank.md`. No invented metrics, scope, titles, or dates.
2. No em-dashes. Commas, colons, and periods only. Strip hidden Unicode (narrow no-break space, zero-width, BOM).
3. Fixed facts are immutable: companies, employment dates, titles held, education, clearance, contact.
4. One page unless the role clearly warrants two (very senior or deep government). Cut the weakest-fit bullets first.

## Bullet craft
- Outcome before activity. Lead with the result and the number, then how. "Closed the company's first 7-figure deals and scaled ARR 30 to 40 percent annually" beats "Responsible for sales".
- Isolate the candidate's contribution. "Co-built", "led product on", "owned", accurate to the bank. Technical-leader readers flag "the work that led to $120M" as borrowed credit; prefer "led product on the prototype that proved X, which unlocked $120M+".
- Vary the opening verb. Do not stack Spearheaded, Leveraged, Drove, Orchestrated down the page; the repetition is the top AI-resume tell. Mix natural verbs: Built, Led, Owned, Closed, Ran, Shaped, Kicked off.
- Mirror JD vocabulary truthfully. Use the JD's term for the real thing the candidate did.

## AI-resume tells to avoid
- Stacked identical verb-first cadence on every line.
- Filler phrases: "results-driven", "proven track record", "results-oriented team player", "dynamic leader", "strategic thinker", "detail-oriented", "self-starter", "excellent communication skills", "leveraged synergies", "spearheaded cross-functional initiatives".
- Responsibilities listed with no number under a big claim.
- Hype vocab: leverage, robust, seamless, spearheaded, pivotal, holistic, best-in-class.
- Left-in artifacts like "[add number here]".

## Voice
Direct, concrete, technical, no corporate filler. Confident without bragging. A bullet should read like something a sharp operator wrote, not a template. Match the voice and tone captured in `config/profile.md`.

## Sensitivity
Keep classified or proprietary specifics vague: no program names, no classified capability detail. Referencing a current or former employer by name is fine; never reproduce an employer's proprietary material. Honor anything the candidate flagged as sensitive in `config/profile.md`.
