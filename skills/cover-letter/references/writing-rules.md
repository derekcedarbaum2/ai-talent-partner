# Writing rules : voice and hard gates

These rules govern the draft and every revision. They are not suggestions.

## Hard gates (a violation fails the artifact)
1. No em-dashes (U+2014). Use commas, colons, or periods. Also strip en-dashes used as em-dashes and any hidden Unicode (narrow no-break space, zero-width, BOM).
2. No fabricated facts or metrics. Every claim traces to the accomplishment bank, a prior letter, or the user's interview answers. Never write "measurably" or "significantly" without an actual number; if you do not have one, describe the mechanism instead.
3. 250 to 400 words, one page. Over-length is the default failure.
4. Run the AI-tells sweep (below) before saving.

## The candidate's voice
- Direct, first-principles, punchy. Read `config/profile.md` for the candidate's stated voice and tone, and match it. A cover letter stays professional but keeps the directness.
- Active voice, named agents. Cut filler adverbs. Split long sentences. Apply this up front, not as a polish pass.
- No corporate tone, no HR-speak, no hedging, no press-release language.
- Match the candidate's level: technical, not over-explained.

## AI-writing tells to strip (highest-signal)
- Negative parallelism: "It's not X, it's Y" or "not just X but Y". Just say what it is.
- Tricolons on autopilot: three stacked adjectives. Real enumerations (a list of functions from the JD) are fine; rhetorical threes are not.
- Copula avoidance: "serves as", "stands as a testament", "boasts", "leverages". Use "is" and "has".
- Participial significance tails: ", underscoring its importance" or "showcasing X". Delete, or make it a real sentence.
- Hype and puffery vocab: delve, leverage, robust, seamless, pivotal, navigate the complexities, unlock, elevate, spearheaded, results-driven, proven track record.
- Bold lead-in bullets, emoji markers, "In today's" openers, "It's worth noting" hedges, tidy recap conclusions.
- Resume tells specifically: every line opening with the same verb cadence (Spearheaded, Leveraged, Drove); responsibilities listed instead of quantified outcomes.

## Structure checklist (per draft)
- Opener is a quantified credential or a sharp company-specific insight, not a cliche opener.
- The proof paragraph is built on the JD's CORE competency and tells one challenge to action to result story.
- The candidate's specific contribution is isolated, not laundered through team or company outcomes.
- At least one decision with a named cost or tradeoff, or a concrete root-cause or build story.
- Why-this-company is in the candidate's words, evidence-backed, no tagline echo.
- A differentiator paragraph ties to an explicit JD bet, showing a built thing, not a volume brag.
- Close is confident and forward; no throat-clearing.
- Em-dash, AI-tells, and word-count sweep passed.

## Self-check before every save
Run mentally: "Could this exact letter be sent to another company?" If yes, it is too generic; add specifics. "Does any sentence brag without proof?" Replace with a built thing and a result. "Did I quote their marketing?" Rewrite in the candidate's voice.
