# sense-of-style : Antipatterns

## 1. Flagging bogus "rules"

What: marking split infinitives, ending with prepositions, starting with "and" or "but", singular "they", or contractions as errors.

Fix: Pinker's bogus-rules list. These are folklore, not grammar. NEVER flag them.

## 2. Over-pruning for "word economy"

What: cutting content in half, losing argument or nuance to hit a word target.

Fix: target 15 to 30 percent word reduction. Cut filler, not substance. If a cut loses meaning, do not cut.

## 3. Flagging active voice where passive is correct

What: "The data was analyzed by the team." to "The team analyzed the data."

But sometimes passive is correct: "The patient was examined" (the agent is unimportant). Or: "Three trials have been published" (the emphasis is on the published work, not the publisher).

Fix: prefer active voice, but passive is valid when the agent is genuinely unimportant or when emphasis should land on the object.

## 4. Rewriting in a neutral AI voice instead of the candidate's voice

What: producing rewrites that sound neutral or academic.

Fix: match the candidate's voice as captured in the workspace profile.md (config key profile_file): direct, first-principles, no corporate polish.

## 5. Ignoring context

What: applying the same style rules to a resume bullet and a long-form essay.

Fix: style rules scale by register. A resume bullet has different rules than a strategy brief.

## 6. Scoring everything "classic style"

What: treating every dimension as the same check.

Fix: 9 separate dimensions, each scored independently. Do not let one failing dimension drag the others.

## 7. No rewrite, just diagnosis

What: listing problems without producing a revised version.

Fix: diagnosis plus rewrite, always (unless the user asked for diagnostic only). The reader wants the before AND the after.
